Compare / θ / and / s /

/ θ / consonant can also can be confused with / s / consonant. When you pronounce / θ / make sure that you put the tip of the tongue close to the upper front teeth whereas when you pronounce / s / the tip of the tongue should be close to the middle of the ridge behind the front teeth. Practice the following words and tongue twisters paying particular attention to the difference in the position of the tongue for / θ / and / s /.

Compare / θ / and / f /

/ θ / consonant sometimes is confused with / f / consonant. An important difference in the pronunciation of these two consonants is that for / θ / your tongue tip is close to the front teeth, whereas for / f / your bottom lip touches the front teeth. Listen carefully to the following pairs of words and tongue twisters to pick up the difference between / θ / and / f /.

Word pairs

thirst /θɜ:st/ – first /fɜ:st/

thin /θɪn/ - fin /fɪn/

three /θri:/ – free /fri:/

Tongue twisters

Compare / ð / and / z /

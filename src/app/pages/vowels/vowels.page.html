/ ʌ / vowel

Description

/ ʌ / is a short vowel. You pronounce it as if you intend to pronounce /ɑː/ but then suddenly lift the back of the tongue towards the soft palate.

Practice

rum /rʌm/ come /kʌm/ sucks /sʌks/
rough /rʌf/ ton /tʌn/ pun /pʌn/
much /mʌtʃ/ bus /bʌs/ love /lʌv/
mother /mʌðə/ cup /kʌp/ enough /ɪˈnʌf/

Written Form of / ʌ /

u before consonant pun, rum, rust, just

o before m, v, th come, love, mother

ou + gh rough, tough, enough

/ɑː/ vowel

Description

/ɑː/ is a long vowel. You pronounce it with your mouth open, as if a doctor asks you to open your mouth to see your throat. Keep lips rounded and the tip of the tongue far away from the bottom teeth.

Practice

farm /fɑːm/ mark /mɑːrk/ father /fɑːðə/
class /klɑːs/ charm /tʃɑːm/ pass /pɑːs/
lark /lɑːk/ bark /bɑːk/ calm /kɑːm/
spark /spɑːk/ part /pɑːt/ plant /plɑːnt/

WRITTEN FORM OF /ɑː/

a + r mark, dark, lark

a before f, nt, th raft, father, plant

a + s before consonant pass, ask, past

a + l calm, balm,almond, palm

i: vowel

Description

i: is a long vowel. The tongue is moved forward, the tip of the tongue is close to the bottom teeth and the lips are in a smile position.

Practice

lead /li:d/ fleece /fli:s/ key /ki:/
sea /si:/ wheat /wi:t/ canteen /kænti:n/
meat /mi:t/ reach /ri:tʃ/
peel /pi:l/ receive /rɪˈsi:v/ cheap /tʃi:p/

Written Form of i:

Main Rules

e + a real, leak, cheap

e + e sheet, peel

Note: peace and piece, sea or see, meet or meat are spelt in the same way, but have different meanings. Peace means the lack of war, whereas piece means a part of an object. Different meanings also have leak and leek, heal and heel, meat and meet, lead and leed.

/ ɪ / vowel

Description

The pronunciation of / ɪ / is very similar to / i :/. However, / ɪ / is much shorter sound. Start to pronounce / ɪ / as if you intend to pronounce / i:/, but then immediately stop. The stop should be energetic to make / ɪ / sound differently from / i:/.

Practice

list /lɪst/ ink /ɪŋk/ insist /ɪnˈsɪst/
trip /trɪp/ bin /bɪn/ trim /trɪm/
miss /mɪs/ pill /pɪl/ fin /fɪn/
it /ɪt/ this /ðɪs/ hill /hɪl/

Written Form of / ɪ /

Main Rules

i + double consonant or consonant at the end of a word hill, lick exception: live

y+ double consonant or consonant at the end of a word gym, symbol

e in unstressed position event /i’vent/, relax /ri’læks/

/u:/ vowel

Description

/u:/ is a long vowel. The tongue is pulled away from the lower front teeth and the lips are slightly rounded.

Practice

Written Form of /u:/

Main Rules

u+ consonant+ vowel rune, superman, lupin

/ɔ:/ vowel

Description

/ɔ:/ is a long vowel. Slightly round your lips and pull the tongue back about one third away from the front teeth. Don’t forget to keep this sound long.

Practice

Written Form of /ɔ:/

Main Rules

a + l walk, talk, call

o + r order, orchard

a + u autumn, author; exception: aunt

a + w (in the end of words) flaw, claw, law

ough in the past tense of verbs thought, sought, fought, bought

/ɒ/ vowel

Description

/ɒ/ is a short equivalent of /ɔ:/. Lips and tongue are in a very similar position to the one for /ɔ:/ - lips are slightly rounded and the tongue is pulled away back about one third away from the front teeth. However, keep this sound short to distinguish it from /ɔ:/ .

Practice

Main Rule

/3:/ vowel

Description

/3:/ is a long vowel that is quite unique to English . It is likely that you will not have this sound in your language. Lift the back of the tongue, keep teeth close together and smile as you pronounce this vowel.

Practice

firm /fɜ:rm/ turn /tɜ:n/ girl /gɜ:l/
burn /bɜ:n/ learn /lɜ:n/ purple /pɜ:pl/
heard /hɜ:d/ bird /bɜ:d/ earn /ɜ:n/
dirty /dɜ:ti/ thirty /θɜ:ti/ concern /kənsɜ:n/

WRITTEN FORM OF /3:/

er + consonant verb, herb

ir + consonant bird, third

ur + consonant turn, burn

ear + consonant learn, earth, early

/ə/ vowel

Description

/ə/ is the most common English vowel. It has a special name – shwa and always occurs in unstressed position in the beginning, in the middle or in the end of words. Listen carefully and try to catch /ə/ in following examples:

Practice

pilot /‘pailət/ agree /əg’ri:/ collar /‘kɔlə/
entertain /entə’tein/ approve /ə’pru:v/ characters/‘kærikəz/
dinners /‘dinəz/ adore /ə’dɔ:/ butter /‘bʌtə/
account /ə’kaunt/ sooner /‘su:nə/ standard /‘stændəd/

WRITTEN FORM OF /ə/

The sound / ə / can be represented by a wide variety of letters and the combination of letters. A common rule, however, is that this sound always occurs in the unstressed part of a word. The most common letters and combinations of letters which represent /ə / are:

e + r perhaps /pəh’æps/

a+r wizard /‘wizəd/

o + u glamorous /‘glæmərəs/

o + r sailor/‘seilə/

o + u + r colour /‘kʌlə/

e + r + e hindered /‘hindəd/

o pilot /‘pailət/

/e/ vowel

Description

The sound /e/ is a short vowel. Put your lips in a smile position, open your mouth, but not very wide, and keep your tongue slightly raised.The tip of the tongue is touching front teeth of the lower jaw.

Practice

led /led/ net /net/ telex /teleks/
bed /bed/ wet /wet/ peck /pek/
send /send/ check /tʃek/ flesh /fleʃ/
kettle /ketl/ neck /nek/ bet /bet/

WRITTEN FORM OF / e /

e + consonant in a stressed position: let, desk, bed, end, tent

/ æ / vowel

Description

/æ/ is a short vowel. The pronunciation of /æ/ is very similar to /e/ - put your lips in a smile position and open your mouth, but not very wide. The lower jaw for /æ/,however, moves more energetically down then for /e/ and the tongue is kept flat in the mouth. The tip of the tongue is pointing towards the alveolar ridge, close to the bottom of front teeth.

Practice

bag /bæg/ family /ʃæmili/ land /lænd/
hat /hæt/ and /ænd/ tan /tæn/
plan /plæn/ can /kæn/ mash /mæʃ/
thank /θænk/ sand /sænd/ pack /pæk/

WRITTEN FORM OF /æ/

a+ consonant in a stressed position narrow, map, add
